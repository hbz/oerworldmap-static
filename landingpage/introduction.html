What is OER?

Open Educational Resources (OER) describes learning and teaching materials that are openly licensed or otherwise in the public domain. They can be used, adapted, and redistributed freely without any need to secure additional permissions. OER are a key driver of the Open Education movement.

Why use OER?

OER use is associated with increasing access to education and supportinh innovation in teaching and learning. Many educators use OER for inspiration; to enhance existing educational materials; to ensure students have access to high quality learning resources; or as a tool for curriculum development. Open Education fosters a spirit of sharing and co-operation the building of peace, sustainable social and economic development, and intercultural dialogue. OER contribute to these goals by increasing access to educational material, improving its quality, lowering its costs and facilitatingsupporting educational innovation.

OER World Map?

The goal of the OER World Map is to illuminate the global Open Educational Resources movement by facilitating interaction and collaboration. The map dors this by sharing open data about activities involving OER. Many different use cases are supported. These include providing information about innovations and impact involving OER; examples of best practice; help with finding OER relevant to a particular context; ways to contact experts; where to find conferences and events; providing statistics about OER, and more.It supports a multitude of use cases including sharing stories, finding qualified lists of OER sources and providing statistics.
